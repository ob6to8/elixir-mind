# cognitive debt

**Operator-coined.** The comprehension gap a human accrues toward a system and its content — the analogue of *technical debt* — when automation adds or alters material faster than the operator internalizes it, so their understanding falls behind the system's actual state. Like technical debt it is not failure in itself but a *liability that compounds*: unattended, the operator's mental model drifts further from the corpus with each automated change, until they can no longer reason about what the system holds. It is **user-defined** — the operator sets the bar for what counts as "understood," so the debt is measured against their own intended grasp, not an external standard. Paid down by editorial engagement (reading, pruning, relabeling, merging) rather than by more automation. Central to the [auto-intake model](/meta/plans/auto-intake-featured-news.md), where automated corpus growth is deliberately traded against the operator's capacity to keep up.
