# invisible degradation

Corpus rot that emits no signal while it happens: a stale cross-reference, an unlisted file, or a silently filed near-duplicate changes nothing observable, so nothing distinguishes a healthy knowledge base from a rotting one until retrieval visibly fails — and by then remediation means auditing hundreds of files rather than fixing one. It is the compounding consequence of [probabilistic enforcement](/glossary/probabilistic-enforcement.md) (violations occur but nothing announces them) and the reason this brain's countermeasures are all *announcement* mechanisms: gates that fail, [warn passes](/glossary/warn-pass.md) that report, and the session-init digest that surfaces open work every session start.
