# knowledge-representation

Formalisms for representing knowledge so machines can store, traverse, and
reason over it — dependency/justification structures, logics, and ontology
languages.

## References

- [Truth maintenance systems (TMS / RMS)](/knowledge-management/knowledge-representation/truth-maintenance-systems.md) — Doyle's JTMS and de Kleer's ATMS: beliefs tracked with their justifications in a bipartite dependency graph, revised when premises change.
- [FOL and OWL: fully formal knowledge representation](/knowledge-management/knowledge-representation/first-order-logic-and-owl.md) — the fully formal pole: provable entailment and its decidability/authoring costs.
